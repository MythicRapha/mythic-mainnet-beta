# GOVERNING PRINCIPLES OF MYTHIC FOUNDATION
## A Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association (DUNA)
### Formed Under Wyoming Statute §§ 17-36-101 through 17-36-115

**Effective Date:** February 25, 2026

---

## ARTICLE I — NAME

The name of this decentralized unincorporated nonprofit association is **Mythic Foundation** (the "Association").

---

## ARTICLE II — PURPOSE

The Association is organized and operated exclusively for the following nonprofit purposes:

1. To develop, maintain, govern, and support the Mythic Layer 2 blockchain network and its associated protocols, infrastructure, and ecosystem;
2. To fund and coordinate open-source software development related to the Mythic network;
3. To distribute grants to developers, validators, and contributors building on the Mythic network;
4. To promote education, research, and adoption of decentralized computing and artificial intelligence technologies;
5. To govern the Mythic network protocol parameters, treasury, and upgrade processes through decentralized on-chain governance;
6. To engage in any lawful activity incidental to or in furtherance of the above purposes.

**No part of the net earnings** of the Association shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, administrators, officers, or other private persons, except that the Association shall be authorized to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth herein.

## ARTICLE III — MEMBERSHIP

### 3.1 Qualification
Membership in the Association is open to any person or entity that holds the MYTH governance token on the Mythic L2 network or the Solana L1 network.

- **Mythic L2 (Native):** The native MYTH token used as gas on the Mythic L2 network

### 3.3 Member Count
As of the Effective Date, the Association has approximately 300 members, exceeding the minimum threshold of 100 members required under Wyoming Statute § 17-36-102(a)(i).

### 3.4 Voting Rights
Each member's voting power is proportional to their MYTH token holdings at the time of any governance proposal snapshot. One MYTH token equals one vote.

### 3.5 No Mandatory Obligations
Membership does not impose any financial obligation or personal liability upon members beyond their token holdings. Members may freely transfer their tokens, which constitutes a transfer of membership.

## ARTICLE IV — GOVERNANCE

### 4.1 On-Chain Governance
The Association is governed through on-chain proposals and voting via the Mythic Governance Program deployed at address: **MythGov111111111111111111111111111111111111** on the Mythic L2 network.

### 4.2 Proposal Types
- **Protocol Upgrades:** Changes to network parameters, program upgrades
- **Treasury Expenditures:** Grants, funding allocations, operational expenses
- **Governance Amendments:** Changes to these Governing Principles
- **Administrator Elections:** Appointment or removal of administrators

### 4.3 Voting Thresholds
- **Standard Proposals:** Simple majority (>50%) of participating votes
- **Constitutional Amendments:** Supermajority (>66.7%) of participating votes
- **Emergency Actions:** Administrator discretion with subsequent ratification

### 4.4 Quorum
A minimum of 5% of total circulating MYTH supply must participate in a vote for the result to be binding.

---

## ARTICLE V — ADMINISTRATORS

### 5.1 Initial Administrator
The initial Administrator of the Association is the founding contributor, serving until the first governance election.

### 5.2 Powers
Administrators may:
- Execute day-to-day operations of the Association
- Sign contracts and agreements on behalf of the Association
- Manage treasury operations within approved budgets
- Implement governance decisions
- Respond to emergency situations affecting network security

### 5.3 Limitations
Administrators may NOT:
- Distribute net earnings to members or themselves (beyond reasonable compensation approved by governance)
- Amend these Governing Principles without member approval
- Encumber Association assets without governance authorization exceeding $50,000
- Unilaterally shut down or fundamentally alter the Mythic network

### 5.4 Compensation
Administrator compensation, if any, shall be set by governance vote and shall be reasonable for services rendered.

---

## ARTICLE VI — TREASURY

### 6.1 Sources
The Association treasury is funded by:
- Protocol fees (10% of transaction fees as defined by the MYTH Token Program)
- Bridge fees
- Donations and grants
- Any other revenue generated in furtherance of the Association's purpose

### 6.2 Management
Treasury funds are held in multi-signature wallets requiring approval from designated key holders as appointed by governance.

### 6.3 Expenditures
All expenditures must further the Association's stated purposes. Expenditures over $50,000 require governance approval.

---

## ARTICLE VII — LIABILITY

### 7.1 Limited Liability
In accordance with Wyoming Statute § 17-36-109, a member or administrator is not personally liable for the acts, debts, or obligations of the Association solely by reason of being or acting as a member or administrator.

### 7.2 Indemnification
The Association shall indemnify any administrator, officer, or agent against expenses (including attorneys' fees) actually and reasonably incurred in connection with any action, suit, or proceeding arising from their service to the Association, to the fullest extent permitted by Wyoming law.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — DISSOLUTION

### 8.1 Process
The Association may be dissolved by a supermajority governance vote (>66.7%).

### 8.2 Distribution of Assets
Upon dissolution, all remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively for the purposes set forth in Article II, or to one or more organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or scientific purposes that qualify as exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. No assets shall be distributed to members.

## ARTICLE X — AMENDMENTS

These Governing Principles may be amended by a supermajority (>66.7%) governance vote conducted through the on-chain Governance Program, provided that:

1. The proposed amendment is published for at least 7 days before voting begins;
2. Quorum requirements are met;
3. The amendment does not contravene Wyoming law.
